The Market Opportunity
72 million Americans hold HSA/FSA accounts. Most of them have no idea what they can buy.
Every year, $3.4 billion in FSA funds are forfeited by American workers — not because they don't need health products, but because the system is broken. Employees don't know what's eligible. Letters of Medical Necessity create weeks of friction. And the products they can buy aren't clinically vetted. Beim Health sits at the center of this problem — a zero-fee marketplace that unlocks spending for consumers, drives utilization for employers, and creates revenue for providers.

Investment Thesis

Why Beim Health.
Why now.

Four structural advantages that compound as the platform scales.

01
Massive, Underserved TAM
$116B in HSA assets. $3.4B forfeited annually. 72 million account holders. The HSA/FSA-eligible health product market is large, growing, and has zero dominant marketplace player. Nobody owns the purchase layer between "employee has funds" and "employee buys a product."
02
Multi-Sided Network Effects
More employees attract more employers. More employers attract more brokers. More patients attract more clinics. More clinics improve product recommendations. Each side of the marketplace strengthens the others — creating compounding defensibility that single-sided solutions can't replicate.
03
Zero-CAC Distribution Channels
Brokers distribute Beim to employer clients for free. Employers distribute to employees for free. Clinics distribute to patients via EMR integration. Every channel is incentive-aligned and cost-free — making Beim one of the rare marketplaces where distribution scales without proportional spend.
04
AI + Clinical Moat
Our Clinical Evidence Level (CEL) grading rubric, AI Care Navigators, and automated LMN workflow create switching costs that deepen with every transaction. The more data we process, the better the product matching — and the harder it becomes for a competitor to replicate the clinical layer.
